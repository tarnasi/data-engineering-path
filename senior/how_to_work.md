# Senior Homework Guide

## How To Do The Homework

For every task at this level:

1. Define the business and system risk first.
2. State the architectural options.
3. Choose one option and defend the tradeoffs.
4. Think about reliability, cost, scalability, and team impact.
5. Make the design operable, not just technically impressive.
6. Write clearly enough that another engineer can extend the work.

## What You Must Explain After Each Task

After each task, explain these points:

- what problem is being solved at system level
- what tradeoffs were made and why
- how reliability is protected
- how cost is controlled
- how security or governance is handled
- what the operational failure modes are
- what standards or conventions this task should establish
- how a less experienced engineer could maintain it

## Senior Quality Rules

- No architecture without tradeoffs.
- No platform decision without operational consequences.
- No scale claim without bottleneck analysis.
- No standards proposal without adoption path.
- No incident response without root cause thinking.

## Submission Standard

A task is complete only when you have:

- a clear design or implementation
- a written tradeoff analysis
- operational and reliability reasoning
- team-facing explanation or standard where relevant
- evidence that the design is maintainable
